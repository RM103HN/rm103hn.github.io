Privacy Policy for Absurd Calendar

Effective Date: 5 May 2026
Last Updated: 5 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Absurd Calendar ("Absurd Calendar", the "App", "we", "us", or "our") accesses, uses, stores, and shares information when you install or use the App.

Developer / Publisher: Ritik Morey
Privacy Contact: ritikmorey@gmail.com

1. Scope of This Policy

This Policy applies only to Absurd Calendar. It does not apply to third-party apps, websites, calendar providers, operating-system services, or artificial intelligence services that may be accessed through or used with the App. Those third parties handle information according to their own terms and privacy policies.

2. Information the App Accesses or Stores

Absurd Calendar is a calendar and planning application. Depending on the features you use and the permissions you grant, the App may access or store the following information on your device:

  • Calendar information, including calendar names, calendar account identifiers, event titles, event descriptions, dates, times, time zones, locations, recurrence rules, reminders, event colors, and calendar provider event identifiers.
  • User-created App content, including events, day templates, saved AI summaries, event metadata, colors, layout preferences, hidden calendar selections, default calendar settings, reminder settings, image quality settings, WebView URL preferences, onboarding state, import/sync timestamps, and other App settings.
  • AI prompt content, including natural-language instructions you type into the AI assistant and, where necessary to complete the request, relevant calendar event details.
  • Attachments and media you choose to add, including images imported from your device, images imported from a web URL, file attachment names, file attachment types, file attachment URIs, and persisted read permissions for files you select through the Android file picker.
  • Gemini API key, if you choose to provide one for AI features.
  • Technical information generated by the App locally, such as local database records, scheduling metadata, and notification scheduling information.

The App does not require account registration with us, and we do not operate a separate user account system for Absurd Calendar.

We do not operate our own backend servers to collect, store, or analyze your calendar data or other personal information. Except for third-party services you choose to use, such as Google's Gemini API, calendar account providers, websites, image hosts, or Android system services, App data remains on your device.

3. How Information Is Used

We use information accessed or stored by the App only for App functionality, including to:

  • Display, create, edit, import, synchronize, and delete calendar events.
  • Read from and write to the Android Calendar Provider when you grant calendar permissions.
  • Let you choose calendars, hide calendars, select a default calendar, and manage calendar-specific display preferences.
  • Schedule local event notifications and reminders.
  • Store App settings, themes, templates, attached images, file attachment references, and saved summaries.
  • Generate AI-assisted calendar events, plans, edits, and summaries when you use the AI assistant.
  • Load images from URLs that you choose or select.
  • Display web content in the App's WebView based on the configured WebView URL.
  • Export calendar views to files such as PDF or PNG when you request an export.
  • Restore reminder scheduling after device restart, time changes, time zone changes, app updates, or alarm permission changes.

4. AI Features and Google Gemini

Absurd Calendar includes optional AI-assisted features powered by the Google Gemini API. These features are used only when you choose to use the AI assistant and provide a Gemini API key.

When you use AI features, the App may send the following information directly from your device to Google's Gemini API over an encrypted HTTPS connection:

  • Your AI prompt or calendar instruction.
  • Relevant calendar event details needed to classify, create, edit, plan, or summarize calendar information.
  • Your Gemini API key, sent as an API credential for the request.

The App stores your Gemini API key locally using encrypted Android storage. We do not operate a server that receives or stores your Gemini API key. Google may process Gemini API requests according to Google's applicable terms, policies, and privacy practices.

If you do not provide a Gemini API key or do not use AI features, the App will not send AI prompts or calendar context to the Gemini API.

5. Calendar Provider Access

If you grant calendar permissions, Absurd Calendar may access the Android Calendar Provider to read available calendars and calendar events and to create, update, or delete events on calendars that you select.

Calendar data may also be handled by the calendar account provider connected to your device, such as Google Calendar, an enterprise calendar provider, or another account provider. Such providers process calendar data according to their own privacy policies and account settings.

If you deny calendar permissions, calendar-provider features may be limited or unavailable.

6. Notifications, Alarms, and Device Permissions

Absurd Calendar may request permissions needed for calendar features, reminders, and notifications, including calendar read/write permissions, notification permission, exact alarm permission, vibration, wake lock, internet access, and boot/time-change handling.

These permissions are used to provide App features such as event sync, reminders, notification delivery, AI requests, WebView access, image URL loading, and reminder rescheduling. Notification content may include event-related information, such as an event title or reminder time, so it may be visible on your device according to your notification settings.

7. Attachments, Images, WebView, and External Links

When you attach an image from your device, the App may copy that image into the App's private local storage. When you attach an image from a URL, the App downloads that image from the specified web address and saves a local copy.

When you attach files through the Android file picker, the App may store file metadata and a reference URI so the selected file can be opened later. File access remains subject to Android's file permission system and the permissions you grant.

The App may display web content using a WebView and may open external links, maps, or files through other apps installed on your device. Third-party websites and apps may collect information under their own privacy policies. Absurd Calendar does not control third-party websites or services loaded in the WebView or opened from the App.

8. Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information.

Absurd Calendar may share or disclose information only as needed for the following purposes:

  • With Google's Gemini API, when you use AI features that require Gemini processing.
  • With Android Calendar Provider and the calendar account providers configured on your device, when you grant calendar permissions and use calendar synchronization features.
  • With websites, image hosts, or WebView destinations that you choose to load or use.
  • With other apps on your device when you intentionally open, export, or share a file, attachment, map location, calendar export, or external link.
  • With Android system services, such as notification, alarm, file picker, backup, device-transfer, and permission-management services, as needed for App functionality.
  • If required by applicable law, legal process, or enforceable governmental request.

The App does not include advertising SDKs and does not share user data with advertising networks for targeted advertising.

9. Data Storage, Security, and Backup

Most App data is stored locally on your device using Android storage mechanisms such as a local database, DataStore preferences, private app files, and encrypted preferences for the Gemini API key.

We use reasonable technical measures available on Android to protect stored information, including encrypted local storage for the Gemini API key. Network requests to the Gemini API and image or web URLs are made over the protocols supported by the destination service, and Gemini requests are sent over HTTPS.

No method of storage or transmission is completely secure. You are responsible for maintaining the security of your device, screen lock, device backups, Google account, calendar accounts, and any Gemini API key you provide.

The App currently allows Android backup and device-transfer behavior. Depending on your device settings, Android version, Google account settings, and platform behavior, some App data may be included in device backup or transferred to a new device by Android system services. Calendar data stored by external calendar providers may also be backed up or synchronized by those providers according to their own settings and policies.

10. Data Retention and Deletion

Absurd Calendar retains App data on your device for as long as needed to provide App functionality or until you delete it.

You can remove data in the following ways:

  • Delete events, summaries, templates, attachments, settings, or other content through App features where available.
  • Remove or clear your Gemini API key from the App settings where available.
  • Revoke App permissions through Android system settings.
  • Clear the App's storage through Android system settings.
  • Uninstall the App from your device.
  • Delete or modify calendar events directly in the connected calendar provider or calendar app.

Deleting the App or clearing App storage removes local App data from the device, subject to Android backup, device-transfer, and operating-system behavior. Calendar data that has been synchronized with an external calendar provider may remain with that provider until you delete it according to that provider's controls. Files exported from the App or shared with other apps are not automatically deleted when you delete App data.

11. Your Choices and Controls

You may control the App's access to information by:

  • Granting or denying Android permissions.
  • Choosing whether to provide a Gemini API key.
  • Choosing whether to use AI features.
  • Choosing which calendars are shown or used by the App.
  • Choosing whether to attach images, files, URLs, or external web content.
  • Managing notification visibility and permission settings through Android.
  • Managing backup, sync, and account settings through Android and your calendar providers.

Some App features may not work if required permissions or data access are denied.

12. Children's Privacy

Absurd Calendar is not directed to children under 13 years of age, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the App, please contact us using the privacy contact listed above so the issue can be reviewed.

13. International Data Transfers

When you use third-party services such as Gemini, calendar account providers, websites, WebView destinations, or image hosts, information may be processed in countries other than your country of residence. Such processing is governed by the relevant third party's terms and privacy practices.

14. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will update the effective date or last updated date above and make the revised policy available through the App listing, in the App, or another appropriate location.
